Embrace for a plastic-free Dublin future

Embracing a plastic-free ethos is pivotal for safeguarding our environment. Plastic pollution wreaks havoc on ecosystems, threatening wildlife and human health. It contaminates waterways, soils, and air, perpetuating ecological imbalance.

Pressuring leaders for decisive action is imperative. Strong policies can curb plastic production, promote alternatives, and enforce recycling measures. Our collective voice demands regulations that prioritize environmental protection, fostering a culture of responsibility and innovation. We demand from Dublin´s leaders a Local Treaty on Plastics. Become part of the net-zero challenge: accelerating decarbonization in Dublin

Net zero means that all greenhouse gas emissions produced are counterbalanced by an equal amount of emissions that are eliminated. Achieving this will require rapid decarbonization.

Dublin City Council has approved the local authority climate action plan 2024-2029- Climate Neutral Dublin 2030. It sets out the actions that Dublin City Council is taking to prepare our city and people living here for the known impacts of climate change – flooding, sea level rise, extreme weather events, and drought. Climate Neutral Dublin 2030 sets out how the City Council will mitigate greenhouse gas emissions and contribute the global effort to limit warming to below 1.5°C.

Go green: recycling campaign in Dublin

Ireland is off track to meet mandatory EU recycling targets set to apply from 2025. Recycling rates are not keeping pace with increasing levels of waste generation.

Let's put pressure on Dublin City Council to provide a free household waste collection service to all households and ask for a free of charges recycling centres.
