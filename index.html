Embrace for a plastic-free Dublin future!

Embracing a plastic-free ethos is pivotal for safeguarding our environment. Plastic pollution wreaks havoc on ecosystems, threatening wildlife and human health. It contaminates waterways, soils, and air, perpetuating ecological imbalance. Pressuring leaders for decisive action is imperative. Strong policies can curb plastic production, promote alternatives, and enforce recycling measures. By holding leaders accountable, we catalyze systemic change, shifting towards sustainable practices. Our collective voice demands regulations that prioritize environmental protection, fostering a culture of responsibility and innovation. Urging leaders for action is not just a choice; it's a necessity for the preservation of our planet and the well-being of future generations. We demand from Dublin´s leaders a Local Treaty on Plastics. Sign and help us demand concrete actions! Sign the petition.

Become part of the net-zero challenge: Accelerating decarbonization in Dublin

Net zero means that all greenhouse gas emissions produced are counterbalanced by an equal amount of emissions that are eliminated. Achieving this will require rapid decarbonization. Dublin City Council has approved the local authority climate action plan 2024-2029- Climate Neutral Dublin 2030. It sets out the actions that Dublin City Council is taking to prepare our city and people living here for the known impacts of climate change – fooding, sea level rise, extreme weather events, and drought. Climate Neutral Dublin 2030 sets out how the City Council will mitigate greenhouse gas emissions and contribute the global efort to limit warming to below 1.5°C. Claire Byrne, Chair of Climate Action, Energy and Environment SPC, Dublin City Council said, “The journey ahead is going to be difcult and frustrating at times, but it will also be rewarding. Dublin City Council, as a whole is looking forward to working with everyone to make our home a place where everyone can live cleaner, greener, healthier lives.'' Became a volunteer and join our group to delve deeper into the plan, monitor and help with the actions to be implemented and demand compliance with agreed deadlines.

Go green: recycling campaign in Dublin

Ireland’s economy remains linear with waste generation continuing to rise. Recycling rates are not keeping pace with increasing levels of waste generation. Ireland is of track to meet mandatory EU recycling targets set to apply from 2025 for municipal waste, packaging waste and plastic packaging waste. These targets are set to progress the circular economy by prioritising recycling over energy recovery and landfll. Ireland remains overly reliant on unpredictable export markets with almost 382,000 tonnes of residual waste sent for incineration abroad. :he Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) published in 28th November 2023 the Circular Economy and Waste Statistics Highlights Report 2021. Ireland’s waste generation levels are continuing to rise and Ireland is failing to make sufcient inroads towards key EU recycling targets that apply from 2025 onwards. As Dubliners we want to contribute to increase the recycling rates and thus achieving the EU recycling targets. We understand that the fees charged at the recycling centres and the lack of a policy for free collection of waste to be recycled are factors that contribute to the delay in achieving this goal. Let's put pressure on Dublin City Council to provide a free household waste collection service to all households and ask for a free of charges recycling centres.
